ShapeMasters Challenge


Circle

a circle is like a pizza, a tasty round treat. In math, a circle is a special shape that's perfectly round, just like a pizza on a tray. It's smooth and doesn't have any corners or edges.

Imagine a pizza sitting on a table. The diameter of the pizza is like the distance from one side of the pizza to the other, passing through the center. It's the longest straight line you can draw on the pizza.
In a circle, the diameter is the longest straight line that you can draw from one side to the other, passing through the center. It's like the widest part of the circle.
Imagine the center of the pizza as the starting point. Using the distance from the center to the edge, you can make slices that are all the same length.
In a circle, the radius is the distance from the center to any point on the edge. It's like the "half-diameter" because it's only half the length of the diameter.
